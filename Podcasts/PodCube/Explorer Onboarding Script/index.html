#The PodCube™ Explorer

The PodCube Explorer is your gateway to the future and the past. As the newest member of our PodCube Passenger Program, your duty is to review and catalog audio transmissions from all across space and time and place. We've routed a direct feed of our unencrypted data directly to your Brigistics terminal.

Before you begin, we recommend reviewing the About tab to familiarize yourself with the history of the Blakely-Huffman Spheroid and the corporate culture here at the PodCube™ Research and Innovation Campus.

Your daily operations begin on the Brigistics tab. This dashboard provides aggregate analytics of our connected feeds, geographic distribution data, and quick-access functions to immediately intercept the latest or most temporally relevant audio. To get a quick start on your listening journey, simply select "Random Transmission".

Your primary workspace is the Transmissions tab. Here, you'll find the complete registry of every piece of audio our PodCube™ devices have retrieved across space and time. You can filter, search, and sort these recordings in virtually limitless ways. by the model of PodCube that recorded them, their planet of origin, or even the specific era they were captured.

When you find a transmission that requires your attention, select it to view its full metadata in the Inspector tab. The Brigistics department has already analyzed the audio to determine its origin point, temporal distance from Now, and file integrity. From the Inspector, you can play the file immediately or add it to your active workload.

Your workflow is managed in the Queue tab. This is where you assemble your daily listening assignments. You can easily reorder tracks, shuffle your workload, or adjust the playback speed and volume. If you need a continuous stream of audio to maximize productivity, simply engage Radio Mode, and the system will automatically append a random un-reviewed transmission to your queue whenever it runs dry.

If you curate a particularly interesting sequence of transmissions, you can issue a physical Punchcard from the Cards tab. Printing a Punchcard generates a highly compressed Nano-GUID, allowing you to easily share your curated playlist with other personnel, or back it up to your local drive. You can also use the reader on this tab to scan Punchcards created by others.

During scheduled breaks, you are encouraged to visit the Interactive tab. Here, you can access authorized Productivity Modules to maintain cognitive sharpness between listening sessions.

For advanced personnel, the API tab offers direct access to the system's configuration. Here you can toggle your feed protocols, enable debug diagnostics, and execute raw commands directly into the PodCube middleware.

While you work, the Explorer monitors your performance. The system logs every transmission you listen to from start to finish, tracking your progress in your Personnel Record, located under your user badge in the upper right. Consistent performance may unlock PodCube Online Observation Perks, giving you access to classified audio files, experimental interactive modules, and internal documentation.

Please be aware: prolonged exposure to raw 4D audio can cause temporal desync within your terminal's display hardware. You may notice increased scanlines, color shifting, or chromatic aberration the longer you work. This is perfectly normal. If the degradation becomes a hindrance to your productivity, consult the emergency maintenance hatch at the bottom of your Personnel Profile to initiate a manual de-frigulation sequence.

Thank you for your dedication to the PodCube mission. The future is yesterday.
